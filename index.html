Alirio Díaz

Alirio Días was a musician and one of the most important figures of Venezuelan classical guitar.

He was born in Carora, Lara State, Venezuela; November 12, 1923.

From an early age he received lessons in theory, music theory, saxophone and clarinet, under the direction of the well-known teacher, composer and band director, Laudelino Mejías in the state of Trujillo, Venezuela.

In 1945 he entered the "José Ángel Lamas" Superior School of Music, under the tutelage of teachers Pedro A. Ramos, Juan Bautista Plaza, Raúl Borges, Vicente Emilio Sojo and Primo Moschini.

He was welcomed as a clarinetist in the Banda Marcial Caracas, directed by maestro Pedro Elías Gutiérrez, and maestro Vicente Emilio Sojo included him in the ranks of the tenors of the Orfeón Lamas.

In 1950, he became known through recitals on radio stations, private and public venues, at the headquarters of the "José Ángel Lamas" Academy, at the National Library of Caracas and at the Athenaeums of Valencia, Barquisimeto and Trujillo.

Since then, he has performed the best of his repertoire on the guitar, including the works of notable Venezuelan composers such as Borges, Sojo and Antonio Lauro. He participated in the great concert in commemoration of the bicentenary of the death of Johann Sebastian Bach that was held in May 1950 at the Higher School of Music. Alirio Díaz gave a performance of the German composer's Chaconne in this concert.

He then traveled to Europe to carry out artistic postgraduate studies.

While advancing with his studies, he performs with great success in important centers such as the Ateneo de Madrid, the Teatro Español, the Palau de la Música in Barcelona, ​​the Alhambra in Granada, and the Teatro Principal in Valencia.

In 1951 he traveled to Siena, Italy and participated in the high improvement courses in the Chigiana Academy classrooms taught by Andrés Segovia, and he became the best student of the Academy, which was equivalent to considering him as the best promise of the Academy. guitar in the world A couple of years later, Alirio Díaz became Segovia's assistant and substitute at the Academy itself.

Since then he has made many artistic tours throughout the five continents performing as a soloist and accompanied by symphonic orchestras under the direction of Sergiu Celibidache, Leopold Stokowski, Antonio Estévez, Andre Kostelanetz, Rafael Frühbeck de Burgos and José Iturbi, among others.

During these activities, Alirio became aware of the great value of popular musical manifestations on his trips to Venezuela, he dedicated much of his time to the compilation of songs of popular origin, many of which, after guitar harmonizations, were and are interpreted before audiences around the world. Editions and record recordings remain of the same.

In 1956, Alirio Díaz signed his first recording contract with the French company "Éditions de la Boite á Music" with which he recorded his first two LPs. Subsequently, he makes contracts with other companies both in Venezuela and abroad, making recordings, some of them currently digitized.

After many years of artistic career, Alirio Díaz dies in the city of Rome, Italy on July 5, 2016, leaving an impressive legacy as one of the most important classical guitarists in the world.
